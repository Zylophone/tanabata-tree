The Story Behind tanabata-tree

tanabata-tree started out with a simple purpose: to solve the problem of needing to constantly email myself links that I wanted to read later. In hindsight, the solution seemed easy enough. I was to build something similar to Pocket where links, called tanzakus, are stored and can be accessed later. When viewed, tanzakus would automatically be marked as "archived" so as to keep the main feed less populated. Despite some technical challenges at times, that same principal logic remains and is what has allowed tanabata-tree to be one of the best save-it-later applications around. tanabata-tree has also served as a learning platform - allowing me to experiment and try new programming techniques on a real-world application. Due to the theming, I've also learned a little about Japanese culture as well! With this, I hope to continue the development of this beautiful creation as well as help others solve a simple problem that they may face too.
- Cedric Amaya, developer
